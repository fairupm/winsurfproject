Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is the largest and most well-preserved fort in Kerala. This magnificent 17th-century structure offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Arabian Sea. Its unique keyhole shape, massive laterite walls, and the observation tower make it a significant historical monument and a major tourist attraction.

Kappil Beach

Kappil Beach is a serene and relatively unexplored destination, perfect for those seeking tranquility. The beach is known for its stunning views where the golden sands meet the azure waters of the Arabian Sea. The nearby Kodi Cliff offers a panoramic vista of the sea and the backwaters.

Thalangara Harbour

Thalangara is a vibrant part of Kasaragod town, known for its bustling fishing harbour and the historic Malik Dinar Juma Masjid. The area's economy is significantly supported by remittances from expatriates. It's a place where you can witness the local life and culture of Kasaragod.

Ananthapuram Lake Temple

This 9th-century temple is the only lake temple in Kerala and is the original seat (moolasthanam) of Ananthapadmanabha Swami. The temple is situated in the middle of a beautiful lake and is guarded by a vegetarian crocodile named Babia. The intricate wood carvings and the serene atmosphere make it a unique spiritual destination.
